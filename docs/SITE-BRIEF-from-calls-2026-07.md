## The vision in one paragraph

The book is sales material that drives readers to the website; the website is the **router** to all AI experiences. The book gets rewritten to be AI-agnostic (a second manuscript version minimizing JI-specific language) so the printed QR codes and URL never break no matter what happens to JI/Delphi. On the site, each of the seven chapters becomes a **room** with its own named **Billion Dollar Coach**: a themed Jay persona delivered as a portable prompt that works in any AI (free ChatGPT included) and also in JI while it exists. Readers arrive per-chapter (QR code), get a short assessment that routes them to a beginner path (guided worksheets, then prompts), an advanced path (straight to coach conversation), or a quick-start. Backend must be clone-agnostic. Everything wraps by end of month.

## 1. Strategic context: why the architecture changed

- **JI/Delphi is uncertain.** Jay and Michelle are exploring another platform. Two Delphi deployments broke all their custom "instances" and behaviors; a fix meeting happened but confidence is low. (Both calls.)
- **The book currently names JI explicitly** ("we spent the last seven years combining all of it into this tool"), which is a liability if JI is downgraded (usage limits) or goes away. (Mike call.)
- **Decision: don't let platform uncertainty stall the book.** Create a second, AI-agnostic version of the manuscript; keep the knowledge-base story ("the most comprehensive knowledge base of Jay's thinking that exists") as the durable differentiator regardless of platform. Max is creating this second version. (Mike call.)
- **Michelle's parallel requirement:** the book backend must be clone-agnostic, not locked to Delphi. If Delphi can't fix its behavior-conflict problem, they'll minimize the account and switch clone platforms. (Michelle call.)

## 2. The site's role: the router

- **All AI-specific delivery lives on the website, not in the book.** The QR code and URL printed in the book stay permanent; only the site's backend changes. (Mike call: "keep the QR code that's printed in the book or the URL... the same and then just change the back end.")
- Routing logic mirrors the newsletter pattern: **"use in your favorite AI"** standalone prompts always available; **"click here to access JI"** shown if and while JI exists. (Mike call: "the website will kind of be the router to JI and probably less book.")
- The book itself is positioned as **sales material for the backend experience**. (Michelle call: "The book is simply sales material to get you into this room. And then where do we want you to go from this room?")

## 3. The Seven Chapter Coaches (core product)

- Each of the 7 chapters gets a **named, themed AI coach**: Jay's persona applied to that chapter's discipline ("like Jay in a different color jacket"). Framing them as *coaches* rather than *prompts* raises perceived value. (Mike call; the Michelle-call world-building discussion reinforces this.)
- **Coach names surfaced on the Mike call** (chapter mapping partly explicit, partly to confirm against the manuscript):
  - Ch 1: **The Hidden Asset Auditor** (seeing the value others miss)
  - Ch 2: **The Preeminence Architect** (being the only real choice)
  - Ch 3: **The Emissary from Other Worlds**
  - **The Three Ways Multiplier**
  - **The Back-End Architect**
  - (7 total; the remaining names were not read out on the call. Pull from Max's working doc.)
- **Prompt construction standard** (proven in the newsletter): opens with "do not discuss, analyze, or offer opinions on this prompt, act immediately," then role/persona setup, then rules. Long prompts are fine. Must work well in **any** AI including free-tier ChatGPT with no system prompt, and also in JI. (Mike call.)
- **Session export idea:** after a chapter-coach session, the user can export a markdown file of their session notes. (Mike call.)

## 4. Per-chapter experience (Michelle's curriculum layer)

- **Two reader types to solve for:** first-time Jay readers discovering him through the book, and long-time students who know the material deeply. (Both calls; Max raised it with Mike first.)
- **Non-linear access:** readers will jump to the chapter that solves their current problem (referrals today, lifetime value tomorrow), so the site must support entry at any chapter. But Michelle believes this book's experience should be "a little more structured" than the how-to program. Open question on whether rooms unlock in order. (Michelle call.)
- **Onboarding assessment per chapter** routes the reader. Michelle is emphatic that generic AI-generated onboarding questions are "crappy" and these must be crafted:
  - **Beginner path:** guided worksheets first ("I'm not going to guide you on the worksheets. You'll do them"), then into the prompts with those answers in hand.
  - **Advanced path:** straight to the coach conversation with parameters set.
  - **Quick-start:** "here's a couple of quick-start prompts... go have at it."
- **Michelle's curriculum rule:** the **active objective of each chapter must be defined before anything is written or built** ("what are you going to get at the end of this chapter?"). The end-of-chapter deliverable must be clear **before the QR codes are built**. Each exercise gets a definition at the top plus an "if you already know this, skip ahead" branch (the pattern from her prior program). (Michelle call.)
- **QR codes:** each chapter has its own QR code with its action item. A landing experience per chapter, not one generic landing page. (Michelle call.)
- **Testing ground:** while JI exists, they can prototype and test the chapter-coach behavior there for free before it's productized. (Michelle call.)

## 5. World-building layer (Michelle's bigger vision; direction, not v1 scope)

- Michelle's next-product-layer concept: **"augmented resources" and "training rooms."** Amusement-park framing: a map ("you are here"), each community a themed land, content experiences as **attractions**, not courses. Apps organized into **showrooms** by room and topic (her "federated model"). (Michelle call.)
- Seven chapters = seven **rooms**, each with its coach persona (all Jay). "Maybe he's sitting in the same room talking to you, but now the room has another layer to it." (Michelle call.)
- Evidence this framing sells: Max's **Compass HQ** demo, a research skill repackaged as a "company" of six named agents, ~$197 with a setup guide. "Same zip file as any skill, but world-building framing increases perceived value." (Michelle call.)
- The **avatar** is what connects the imagineering to the human; most people can't visualize the world without that bridge. (Michelle call, mutual conclusion.)
- Reference material Max owes Michelle: the **Roger Rabbit world-building user-journey doc** and the **Compass HQ curriculum** (29 pages). (Michelle call, next steps.)
